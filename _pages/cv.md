## Education

Texas Tech University, Lubbock, TX. May 2019. GPA 3.957/4.00. Advisor: Dr. Chuck West.

## Professional Experience

**Assistant Research Professor** — Mississippi State University, Starkville, MS (December 2023 – Present)
- Lead soil-water-plant-atmospheric research, including agrohydrology and agroecology.
- Identify key factors affecting biogeochemical processes in agricultural systems.
- Mentor graduate and undergraduate students, publish data, and secure and manage grants.
- Cultivate and manage strategic relationships with MSU's diverse stakeholders.

**Research Scientist** — Rodale Institute, Kutztown, PA (January 2022 – November 2023)
- Developed row crop and weed management program in regenerative organic systems.
- Oversaw funded projects, managed external grants, and secured nearly $0.5M in grants.
- Published peer-reviewed papers, a book chapter, and web articles on organic crops.
- Led outreach activities including field days, webinars, farm tours, and workshops.

**Postdoctoral Associate** — USDA-Agricultural Research Service, Stoneville, MS (June 2019 – December 2021)
- Managed Long-Term Agroecosystem Research (LTAR) in cotton and sorghum production.
- Published high-impact peer-reviewed articles on soil hydrology and remote sensing.

**Graduate Research Assistant** — Texas Tech University, Lubbock, TX (August 2015 – May 2019)
- Determined water footprint for crude protein and digestible organic matter production in alfalfa-native grass mixtures for the Southern High Plains region of Texas.
- Developed population decay models for alfalfa and calibration parameters for the PR2/6 sensor.
- Published high-impact research papers (two outstanding-paper awards) in peer-reviewed journals.

**Horticulture Development Officer** — Ministry of Agriculture Development, Udayapur and Kathmandu, Nepal (2011 – 2015)
- Designed and executed farmers' education programs including training, extension materials, translational research and demonstration, and on-call services.
- Supervised field extension agents and led capacity-building trainings.

**Lecturer** — Tribhuvan University, Institute of Agriculture and Animal Science, Baitadi, Nepal (2010 – 2011)
- Taught undergraduate courses including Principles of Agronomy (AGR 111), Seed Technology (AGR 212), and Introductory Soil Science (SOL 211).
